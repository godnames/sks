Exploring Mathura: A Spiritual Journey to the Birthplace of Lord Krishna

"If you're staying at the SKS Grand Palace hotel, both of these temples are easily accessible and definitely worth a visit. You can immerse yourself in the rich history and culture of Mathura, and experience the spiritual significance of these holy sites. Additionally, there are many other things to do in Mathura, such as visiting the Dwarkadhish Temple, the Vishram Ghat, and the Mathura Museum, all of which offer unique insights into the city's heritage and traditions."

Shri Krishna Janmasthan Temple, Mathura

4.7 (8,418) Famed Hindu temple believed to be the location where Lord Shri Krishna first manifested.

Distance: 6.1 km.


Banke Bihari Temple

This Rajasthani-style Hindu temple dedicated to Lord Krishna was built in the 19th century.

Distance: 3.1 km.


Iskcon temple

ISKCON (International Society for Krishna Consciousness) temple is a spiritual organization based on the teachings of Bhagavad Gita and Lord Krishna. The temples are known for their devotional music, food, and spiritual practices, and they attract followers from all over the world.

Distance: 5 KM


Prem Mandir

The temple is known for its beautiful white marble architecture, intricate carvings, and stunning lighting displays. It attracts thousands of devotees and tourists every day who come to offer their prayers and witness the spectacular evening aarti ceremony.

Distance: 6 KM


Vaishno Devi Temple

The temple is also known for its stunning natural beauty, serene surroundings, and the challenging trek, which makes it a unique and memorable spiritual experience for the devotees.

Distance: 7 KM


Radha Vallabh Temple

The temple is dedicated to Lord Krishna and Radha, and it is known for its intricate carvings, stunning architecture, and vibrant festivals that attract devotees from all over the world.

Distance: 6 KM


Rang Ji Temple

The temple is dedicated to Lord Rangji, a form of Lord Vishnu, and is known for its impressive South Indian-style architecture, intricate carvings, and colorful festivals that attract devotees from all over the country.

Distance: 4 KM


Nidhivan Temple

The temple is known for its association with the legend of Lord Krishna and his divine consort Radha, and it is believed to be the place where the divine couple performs their Rasleela dance every night.

Distance: 7 KM


Dwarkadhish Temple

The temple is dedicated to Lord Krishna, who is worshipped here in his form as the king of Dwarka. It is considered one of the Char Dham pilgrimage sites and is visited by thousands of devotees every year.

Distance: 3 KM


kans quila

According to the legend, Kans, the maternal uncle of Lord Krishna, lived in the fort and was eventually defeated by Krishna. The fort's ruins and architecture serve as a reminder of this significant event in Hindu mythology.

Distance: 3 KM


Vishram ghat

It is believed to be the place where Lord Krishna rested and took a break during his pastimes in Vrindavan, and it is now a popular spot for pilgrims to take a dip in the holy river and perform religious rituals.

Distance: 4 KM


Kesi ghat

The ghat is famous for its association with the legend of Lord Krishna, who is said to have killed the demon Kesi here and taken a dip in the river Yamuna.

Distance: 7 KM
